# Gate 1 System
## Group Leader Training Manual
### Version 1.0

---

## 1. Purpose

This manual is written ONLY for Group Leaders.

You are the first line of coordination and accountability for your team.

---

## 2. Your Role as a Group Leader

You are responsible for:
- Supporting editors in your group
- Monitoring group progress and health
- Responding to alerts promptly
- Escalating issues when necessary
- Coordinating with other leaders

**You are NOT an editor.** You do not rename files or edit videos.

---

## 3. Daily Group Leader Duties

### Morning
- Check group dashboard
- Verify team members are online
- Review any overnight issues

### During Operations
- Monitor real-time alerts
- Respond to issues within 15 minutes
- Contact editors when problems arise
- Coordinate fixes with your team

### End of Day
- Review group summary
- Ensure all issues are addressed
- Confirm backup coverage
- Report any concerns

---

## 4. Using the Mobile App

### Dashboard
- View team status at a glance
- See online/offline members
- Track files indexed today
- Monitor open issues

### Issues Tab
- View all group issues
- Filter by status/severity
- Acknowledge issues
- Mark as resolved
- Escalate when needed

### Team Tab
- See all team members
- Check online status
- View individual performance
- Contact via call/WhatsApp

---

## 5. Issue Response Protocol

### When an Issue is Reported

1. **Acknowledge** within 15 minutes
2. **Contact** the editor if clarification needed
3. **Assess** the severity
4. **Coordinate** the fix
5. **Verify** resolution
6. **Close** the issue

### Severity Guidelines

| Severity | Response Time | Action |
|----------|---------------|--------|
| Critical | Immediate | Escalate if not fixable |
| High | 30 minutes | Prioritize resolution |
| Medium | 2 hours | Schedule fix |
| Low | End of day | Address when possible |

---

## 6. Issue Escalation Discipline

Escalate ONLY when:
- Issue repeats multiple times
- Issue affects multiple interviews
- Editor cannot resolve after guidance
- Technical failure (disk, agent, network)
- Group is overwhelmed

### Escalation Flow
```
Editor → Group Leader → QA/Admin
```

**Do not escalate minor issues.** Handle them at your level.

---

## 7. Early SD Removal Alerts

When an editor removes an SD card early:

1. You receive immediate notification
2. Contact the editor
3. Understand the reason
4. Ensure files are recovered if possible
5. Log the incident

**This is not punishment.** It's accountability.

---

## 8. Team Communication

### Best Practices
- Be calm and supportive
- Focus on solutions, not blame
- Provide clear guidance
- Follow up on issues
- Celebrate successes

### Contact Methods
- In-app messaging (preferred)
- WhatsApp for urgent matters
- Phone call for critical issues

---

## 9. What You Must NOT Do

- ❌ Rename files yourself
- ❌ Edit videos
- ❌ Change group membership (Admin only)
- ❌ Ignore alerts
- ❌ Blame editors publicly
- ❌ Escalate everything

---

## 10. Group Leader Conduct

- Be calm under pressure
- Be visible and available
- Be supportive of your team
- Protect your team's dignity
- Lead by example

---

## 11. Group Readiness Confirmation

Before major services, confirm:
- ✅ All editors online
- ✅ Storage checked
- ✅ No unresolved issues
- ✅ Backup disks ready
- ✅ Communication channels working

---

## Quick Reference

| Situation | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| New issue | Acknowledge → Contact → Resolve |
| Early SD removal | Contact editor → Understand → Log |
| Repeated errors | Provide guidance → Escalate if needed |
| Editor offline | Contact via phone → Report if unreachable |
| Critical issue | Immediate escalation |
